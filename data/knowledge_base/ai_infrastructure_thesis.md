# AI Infrastructure Investment Thesis

**Version:** 2026.1
**Owner:** Research, AlphaLens
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-10

## 1. Summary

We believe the AI infrastructure cycle is in its second phase: from
chip-building (2023-2025) toward systems, networking, and inference
monetization (2026-2028). Capex remains the dominant earnings driver
for compute providers; software inflection is now the marginal upside.

## 2. Core theses

### 2.1 Compute is durable, not cyclical (yet)
Hyperscaler capex guidance for 2026 implies +28% YoY growth at the
median, with no major customer guiding cuts. Buybacks have decelerated
in favor of capex, indicating multi-year commitment. We expect at least
two more guidance raises before deceleration concerns become consensus.

### 2.2 Custom silicon is additive, not substitutive
Internal accelerators (TPU, MTIA, Trainium) expand the TAM rather than
displacing merchant silicon. Broadcom's custom ASIC business and
TSMC's foundry mix-shift are the primary beneficiaries. NVIDIA retains
the merchant lead at the high end through at least the Rubin generation.

### 2.3 Inference > training in monetization
Training spend is concentrated; inference scales with usage. Companies
with low-latency inference at the edge (CDN, networking, custom silicon)
are positioned to capture margin as application workloads scale.

### 2.4 Power and cooling are the next bottleneck
Datacenter power deals are now the binding constraint on hyperscaler
expansion. We are watching utilities and renewables names with
contracted PPAs to large hyperscalers as a second-order play.

## 3. Position mapping

| AVGO | Custom ASIC + networking silicon |

## 4. Risks to the thesis

- **Capex digestion** — a single quarter of guide-down at any top-3
  hyperscaler would compress merchant silicon multiples by 20-30%.
- **Geopolitical** — TSMC concentration in Taiwan; export controls on
  GPU shipments to China.
- **Power constraints** — multi-year datacenter delays would push
  realized revenue to the right.
- **Open-source models** — commoditization of inference could compress
  software margins faster than hardware demand grows.

## 5. Triggers to revisit

- Two consecutive quarters of decelerating hyperscaler capex guidance.
- Sustained NVDA datacenter gross margin < 65%.
- Major power deal cancellations or grid-tie delays > 18 months.
- Material change in US export-control regime.
